🗺️ Our Journey

Stages 1 Through 4 & RC Challenge

Project Journey Timeline

This page documents our team's complete journey throughout the IAR 2026 project. From our first research steps in Stage 1 to the exciting RC Robot Challenge, every phase has helped us grow as researchers, engineers, and collaborators. Below you will find a summary of each stage, the track image, and embedded videos of our robot in action.


Stage 1 — Research & Problem Identification

For the first stage, we concentrated on understanding the basics of robotics. We learnt how microcontrollers like Arduino work and studied different types of sensors and actuators that can be used in our project. We also practiced how to create logical flowcharts and basic algorithms for understanding how to control the behavior of a robot before starting to build it.

Stage 2 — Nano Robot Design

In the second stage, we carried out virtual prototyping. We used Tinkercad to design and simulate our Arduino circuits to make sure that every connection was correct. This way, we allowed ourselves to observe our robotics in action in a safe environment, testing the sensors and LEDs in order to make sure they reacted to our first code before moving to the physical design.

Stage 3 — Programming & Algorithm Development

For the third stage, we focused on our skeletal system research. Our team created a model on craft paper to represent the skeletal system and a specific disease, simulating how a Bullbot would deliver treatment. Technically, we explored the advantages of block-based programming and mastered commands like "While" and "Loop" in Rogic to automate the robot's movements. We also utilized additional educational resources to strengthen our coding skills.

Stage 4 — Website & Final Presentation

In this final stage, we totally developed our work, which represented a key part of the skeletal system. This was accomplished through the integration of knowledge gained in previous stages, such as programming and robotics, in conjunction with individual creativity and the responsible use of AI tools for carrying out the stage's activities and finalizing this digital portfolio.
