🦴 Sectional Joint Prototype

Human Knee Anatomy Model

Our Project: Functional Knee Joint Section

We have developed a working anatomical model of the human knee joint that integrates external mechanics with internal biology. This physical model is an educational tool used to visualize how the structural components of the skeletal system interact to allow movement and support weight.


Knee Prototype Component Map

Prototype Component Anatomical Representation
Opened Femur Section Cross-section of the Distal Femur showing internal layers.
Internal Grey Lattice Cancellous Bone (Spongy Bone structure).
Solid Outer Ring Cortical Bone (Compact Bone layer).
Yellow Central Core Simulated Bone Marrow within the medullary canal.
Joint Hinge & Patella Demonstration of the mechanical knee joint motion.
Ligament Strips Representation of the Patellar Ligament for joint stabilization.

The prototype has an interactive distal femur, allowing the observers to see through the surface and understand the inside composition of a long bone. This mechanical model is intended to:

  • Anatomy Visualization: Clearly exposes the Femur, Tibia, Patella, and Fibula structures.
  • Internal Structure: Demonstrates the difference between Cortical (compact) Bone and Cancellous (spongy) Bone.
  • Joint Mobility: Simulates the mechanical function of the patellar interaction and ligament stability.
  • Structural Components: Identifies key internal elements like Bone Marrow and vascularization.
  • Interactive Teaching: Provides a hands-on method to localize specific skeletal injuries or diseases.
